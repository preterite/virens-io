<section class="about-lede">
  <h1>About VIRENS</h1>
  <div class="about-statement">
    <p>VIRENS is an open-source research workflow system for humanities scholars. It integrates macOS applications and automation tools into a unified environment that handles the full research lifecycle — from source capture through publication.</p>
    <p>The system is modular, opinionated, and built on the <a href="https://verdantinquiry.org">Verdant Inquiry</a> methodology: the premise that how you organize your research materials is itself a methodological commitment, not a neutral technical choice.</p>
  </div>
</section>

<div class="reading-column">

<h2>Design Principles</h2>

<div class="principles-grid">
  <div class="principle-card">
    <h3>Modular</h3>
    <p>Start with what you need. Each component works independently and connects through standard protocols. No all-or-nothing commitment required.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="principle-card">
    <h3>Durable</h3>
    <p>Plain text at the core. Markdown notes, BibTeX references, shell scripts. Your research survives any single application's obsolescence.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="principle-card">
    <h3>Automated</h3>
    <p>Routine tasks — filing, renaming, linking, backing up — happen without manual intervention. Your attention stays on ideas, not administration.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="principle-card">
    <h3>Scholarly</h3>
    <p>Designed for academic knowledge work, not corporate productivity. Citation pipelines, reading notes, and publication workflows are first-class concerns.</p>
  </div>
</div>
